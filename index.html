MSBA Competency Framework

A Progressive Development Model for Business Analytics Excellence

About This Framework: This competency framework defines the essential capabilities that MSBA students develop throughout their program. Each competency progresses through three distinct levels—Fundamental, Intermediate, and Advanced—reflecting increasing sophistication, independence, and strategic application.

Design Philosophy: Built on evidence-based pedagogical principles, this framework emphasizes growth mindset, authentic assessment, and real-world application. Students are expected to demonstrate cumulative mastery, with each level building upon and integrating prior learning.

COMPETENCY 1: DATA STORYTELLING

Transforming complex data insights into compelling narratives that drive informed decision-making across diverse stakeholder groups

Fundamental
Building Narrative Foundations
Students construct basic data narratives with clear structure, connect findings to business context, and begin adapting communication for different audiences. They organize information logically and use visualizations to support key points, though translation from technical to business language is still developing.

Learning Outcomes - Students Can:

  • Organize findings into a coherent narrative structure (setup, analysis, conclusion)
  • Connect data observations to relevant business implications and context
  • Create appropriate visualizations that support primary messages
  • Present recommendations with basic justification from data evidence
  • Identify target audience needs and adjust terminology accordingly
  • Distinguish between descriptive reporting and insight generation
Intermediate
Strategic Communication
Students craft compelling, audience-tailored narratives that seamlessly integrate data, visualization, and business strategy. They independently translate complex technical findings into actionable insights, design sophisticated visualizations, and construct well-reasoned recommendations that address stakeholder priorities and concerns.

Learning Outcomes - Students Can:

  • Develop complete story arcs that balance technical rigor with accessibility
  • Design visualizations that reveal patterns and support decision-making
  • Craft actionable recommendations tied to specific business outcomes
  • Adapt communication style, depth, and framing to stakeholder expertise
  • Anticipate and address potential questions or concerns in the narrative
  • Synthesize multiple data sources into a unified, coherent story
  • Use persuasive techniques while maintaining analytical integrity
Advanced
Transformative Influence
Students deliver sophisticated, persuasive narratives that drive strategic decision-making in complex, ambiguous situations. They innovate in visualization design to reveal non-obvious insights, craft multi-layered arguments with implementation roadmaps, and influence organizational direction through exceptional data storytelling that bridges technical excellence and business leadership.

Learning Outcomes - Students Can:

  • Create strategic narratives that preemptively address objections and alternatives
  • Design innovative, publication-quality visualizations that communicate complex relationships
  • Develop comprehensive recommendations with phased implementation plans and success metrics
  • Influence cross-functional stakeholders and facilitate consensus building
  • Navigate politically sensitive or controversial data findings diplomatically
  • Establish narrative frameworks that others can adopt for organizational storytelling
  • Mentor others in effective data communication and presentation skills

COMPETENCY 2: AI INTEGRATION

Understanding and leveraging AI tools appropriately in analytics workflows

Beginning
Basic awareness of AI tools with limited ability to use them effectively. Accepts AI outputs without verification or critical assessment.

Observable Behaviors:

  • Uses simple, generic prompts with inconsistent results
  • Cannot explain AI tool selection rationale
  • Does not validate AI-generated outputs
  • Limited understanding of AI capabilities and limitations
Developing
Can use AI tools for standard tasks with guidance. Beginning to evaluate outputs and understand when AI is appropriate to use.

Observable Behaviors:

  • Creates more specific prompts that yield relevant outputs
  • Compares AI results with traditional methods
  • Identifies obvious errors or inconsistencies in AI outputs
  • Shows growing understanding of appropriate AI use cases
Proficient
Strategically selects and effectively prompts AI tools for various analytics tasks. Critically evaluates outputs and understands tool limitations.

Observable Behaviors:

  • Designs sophisticated prompts that produce high-quality outputs
  • Systematically compares AI and traditional approaches
  • Validates and refines AI outputs using domain knowledge
  • Articulates when AI adds value vs. when traditional methods are preferable
Advanced
Demonstrates advanced AI literacy across multiple tools and contexts. Integrates AI strategically into comprehensive analytics solutions.

Observable Behaviors:

  • Engineers complex prompt sequences for multi-step problems
  • Selects optimal AI tools based on task requirements and constraints
  • Develops frameworks for AI validation and quality assurance
  • Innovates new applications of AI in analytics workflows

COMPETENCY 3: CRITICAL THINKING

Analytical evaluation of data, processes, and business problems

Beginning
Accepts data and findings at face value with minimal questioning. Analysis is descriptive rather than evaluative.

Observable Behaviors:

  • Reports what data shows without questioning quality or completeness
  • Does not identify assumptions or limitations
  • Misses gaps in data or logic
  • Concludes without considering alternative explanations
Developing
Beginning to question data quality and analytical approaches. Identifies some limitations, but the evaluation is inconsistent.

Observable Behaviors:

  • Asks basic questions about data sources and collection methods
  • Identifies obvious data quality issues or limitations
  • Considers alternative interpretations when prompted
  • Shows emerging awareness of analytical assumptions
Proficient
Systematically evaluates data quality, analytical methods, and conclusions. Identifies assumptions, gaps, and alternative perspectives.

Observable Behaviors:

  • Critically assesses data sources, completeness, and reliability
  • Maps data processes and identifies where information is missing
  • Evaluates analytical approaches for appropriateness and rigor
  • Considers multiple explanations and acknowledges uncertainty
Advanced
Demonstrates sophisticated analytical reasoning across complex, ambiguous situations. Develops frameworks for critical evaluation.

Observable Behaviors:

  • Creates systematic evaluation frameworks for data and processes
  • Identifies non-obvious gaps, biases, and confounding factors
  • Synthesizes insights from multiple analytical perspectives
  • Proactively questions assumptions and challenges conventional thinking

COMPETENCY 4: TEAMWORK

Effective collaboration in cross-functional analytics projects

Beginning
Participates minimally in team activities. Works primarily in isolation without considering team goals or member contributions.

Observable Behaviors:

  • Completes only individually assigned tasks
  • Limited communication with team members
  • Does not actively contribute to team discussions or decisions
  • Misses deadlines affecting team progress
Developing
Contributes to team tasks and participates in discussions. Beginning to coordinate with others but collaboration is inconsistent.

Observable Behaviors:

  • Completes assigned tasks and communicates progress
  • Participates in team meetings and shares ideas
  • Responds to team member requests and questions
  • Shows willingness to help but requires direction
Proficient
Actively collaborates with team members, leveraging diverse perspectives. Reliably contributes to team success and helps integrate work products.

Observable Behaviors:

  • Proactively shares knowledge and supports team members
  • Facilitates productive team discussions and decision-making
  • Integrates individual contributions into cohesive deliverables
  • Adapts to different team roles as needed
Advanced
Demonstrates leadership in team dynamics and project coordination. Optimizes team performance and navigates complex interpersonal and project challenges.

Observable Behaviors:

  • Identifies and leverages team members' unique strengths
  • Facilitates resolution of conflicts or obstacles
  • Coordinates complex, multi-faceted team projects
  • Builds team culture that encourages innovation and accountability

IMPLEMENTATION GUIDANCE

Using These Rubrics

For Faculty:

  • Map specific assignments to competency levels based on where students should be in program progression
  • Use rubrics to design assignments that scaffold skill development
  • Provide a rubric in advance so students understand expectations
  • Use consistent language from the rubrics in feedback

For Assessment:

  • Not every assignment needs to assess all observable behaviors—select the most relevant criteria
  • Students should demonstrate proficiency by the capstone, but progression varies by course sequence
  • Consider using rubrics for both formative feedback and summative grading
  • Track competency development across multiple assignments within a course

Progression Expectations

  • ISOM 700–631 (Early): Beginning → Developing
  • ISOM 801–825 (Middle): Developing → Proficient
  • ISOM 827–835–837 (Advanced): Proficient → Advanced

Mapping to Assignments

When identifying assignments for the October 16 meeting, we can consider:

  • Which competency level should students demonstrate in this course?
  • Which observable behaviors can this assignment assess?
  • How does this assignment scaffold to the next course's expectations?
  • What feedback mechanisms will support competency development?
